dc:description "The Nok terracotta statues are remarkable artifacts from the Nok culture, one of Africa's earliest known civilizations, which flourished in what is now Nigeria between approximately 1500 BCE to 500 CE . The Nok people are known for their advanced terracotta production, particularly their intricate sculptures, which are considered some of the oldest known sculptures in sub-Saharan Africa. The Nok terracotta statues likely represent a range of subjects, including humans, animals, and possibly mythical or spiritual beings, reflecting the beliefs, customs, and societal structures of the Nok culture.
